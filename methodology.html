Face Detection and Recognition Module

To detect and recognize the students' face for identification purpose

Face Detection and Recognition

This module consists of three main steps, which are image preprocessing, face detection and face recognition. In image preprocessing, the image captured from the camera will be denoised, resized if necessary, and converted into grayscale to ease the later processing. Haar Cascade Face detection algorithm is then applied to detect the faces in the classroom while the Eigenface and Fisherface approaches will be used to train and recognize student faces. This work combined both Eigenfaces and Fisherfaces algorithms to achieve better performance in face recognition.

Eigenfaces Algorithm

Eigenfaces approach uses Principle Component Analysis (PCA) to find the dimensional direction with the greatest variance between projected data samples, and the orthogonal eigenvectors to the dimension. This approach follows the three basic steps to perform face recognition, where first project all the training samples into PCA subspace, then projects all the query images into PCA subspace and identify by evaluate the closest neighbor between the projected training images and query images.

Fisherfaces Algorithm

Fisherfaces approach uses Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA) method to reduce the variance between image space classes, and tightly group those similar groups together to maximize the separation between different projected classes.

Motion Analysis Module

To track and analyse students' motion such as sitting, standing, concentrating and etc

Motion Analysis

Motion analysis module consist of three main functions, which are body detection, motion tracking and motion recognition. For body detection, we detect upper body and full body of students with cascade classifier and Histogram of Oriented Gradients (HOG) respectively. Both upper and full body is considered in this work because students can be seated or standing in a classroom. In this work, we trained a cascade classifier of human upper body (head and shoulder) and use it to detect the upper body of students in the classroom.

As for the full body, it will be detected by using HOG detector where the object appearance and shape within an image can be described by the distribution of intensity gradients or edge directions. Both upper body and full body detection is then integrated in order to assist human motion tracking and human motion recognition.

In motion tracking, feature-based tracking with Lucas- Kanade sparse optical flow approach is used to determine and track the students’ motion. This method assumes that the displacement of the motion between two images is small and solves the optical flow equations by the least squares criterion. Behavioural Analysis Module

To analyse and evaluate students’ behavior, concentration level and performance

Behavioural Analysis

This module fully utilize the data collected from face recognition and motion analysis to analyze the students’ behavior. Different metrics are proposed to calculate and evaluate the attendance level and concentration level of students.

1) Level of presence in class (Px): Equation (1) show the metric to calculate the level of presence of a student in the class by computing the percentage of total time of a particular student appear on the screen, tx over the class duration T. The purpose of this metric is to determine the percentage of the student appear in front of the camera in a class in order to obtain their attendance status.

2) Attendance status, Ax: Equation (2) evaluate the attendance status of a student. If a student appear in front of the screen for more than 50% of the total duration of a class, then the student is consider as presence in that class and vise versa. The purpose of this evaluation is to ensure that the student who leave the class frequently or sneak out from the class half way will not marked as presence in the class.

3) Concentration level of student, Cs : Equation (3) show the metric to calculate the concentration level of a student in a class by computing the percentage of R1 case (please refer to Table I) of a student, rx over the total number of students upper body detected on the screen, bx. This metric assumes that if a particular student’s face is not recognized on the screen but the upper body is detected on the screen, the student face might be facing other direction which leads to other activities such as playing phone, chatting with friends, or sleeping on the table.

4) Overall concentration level of a class, Coverall: To evaluate the effectiveness of the class, we propose (4) to obtain the overall attention of students in a class by computing the total concentration level of students over the total number of students attended the class. If a student does not attend the class, the student will not affect the overall concentration level of students in a class.
